Herbs

Growing herbs indoors is a great way to give your home a pop of greenery and a fragrance! Herbs thrive off of direct sunlight and plenty of watering.

Basil

Widly used to flavor Italian and Thai cuisine, this plant has over 60 variants and can thrive on patios and windowsills!

  • Sunlight: bright and direct light ~6-8 hours daily
  • Water: water when soil is dry, maintain moist but not soggy soil
  • Soil: rich, loamy, well-draining soil

Rosemary

This plant is used for culinary flavor, medicinal uses, and aromatic oils. It is known for its pungent, piney and woody smell.

  • Sunlight: bright and direct light ~6-8 hours daily
  • Water: 1-2 weeks, once soil is dry
  • Soil: well-draining, light, sandy or loamy soil

Mint

This plant best known for its refreshing and cooling flavor, is a fast-growing plant that thrives in moist environments!

  • Sunlight: bright and direct lght ~6-8 hours daily
  • Water: 1-2 weeks, maintain moist but not waterlogged soil
  • Soil: well-draining, slightly acidic potting mix
