Succulent Plants

Succulents are a diverse group of plants that come in many shapes, sizes and colors. They are drought resistent plants, only needing watered every 1-2 weeks. Great plants for beginners!

Cactus

A drought-resistent plant that has a thick stem designed to store water. These plants often bloom with colorful fragrant flowers that range in colors of red, yellow, and pink.

  • Sunlight: bright and direct light ~6-8 hours daily
  • Water: water only when soil is completely dry ~2-4 weeks
  • Soil: sandy, fast-draining, cactus potting mix

Jade

A popular and low-maintance succulent known for its wood stems and fleshy green leaves. These plants often bloom with small red or pink star-shaped flowers in the winter.

  • Sunlight: bright and indirect light ~4-6 hours daily
  • Water: 2-4 weeks when 1-2 inches of top soil is dry
  • Soil: well-draining cactus or succulent mix

Aloe Vera

Famous for its thick fleshy green leaves that contain a gel used to treat sunburns and wounds, this plant is known as the "plant of immortality" due to its fast growing abilities.

  • Sunlight: bright and indirect light at least 6 hours a day
  • Water: water only when soil is completely dry ~2-4 weeks
  • Soil: sandy, fast-draining cactus or succulent potting mix
